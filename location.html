Driving Directions

Easy access from major Ontario cities

From Toronto (3 hours)

Distance: 290 km (180 miles)

  1. Take Highway 400 North from Toronto
  2. Continue on Highway 11 North toward North Bay
  3. Exit at Kearney/Magnetawan (Exit 279)
  4. Turn left on Highway 518 West
  5. Continue for 8 km to Lakeview Road
  6. Turn right on Lakeview Road - MysRetreat is 2 km ahead

Travel Tip: Best departure times are early morning or early afternoon to avoid Toronto traffic.

From Ottawa (3.5 hours)

Distance: 350 km (217 miles)

  1. Take Highway 417 West to Highway 17 West
  2. Follow Highway 17 West to Pembroke
  3. Take Highway 41 North to Dacre
  4. Continue on Highway 132 West to Bancroft
  5. Take Highway 28 North to Highway 518
  6. Follow Highway 518 East to Lakeview Road

Scenic Route: This route takes you through beautiful Madawaska Valley countryside.

From North Bay (1.5 hours)

Distance: 120 km (75 miles)

  1. Take Highway 11 South from North Bay
  2. Continue for 100 km to Kearney exit
  3. Exit at Kearney/Magnetawan (Exit 279)
  4. Turn right on Highway 518 West
  5. Continue for 8 km to Lakeview Road
  6. Turn right on Lakeview Road - destination ahead

Note: This is the most direct route with excellent highway conditions year-round.

From Sudbury (2.5 hours)

Distance: 200 km (124 miles)

  1. Take Highway 69 South toward Parry Sound
  2. Exit onto Highway 637 East to Highway 64
  3. Follow Highway 64 East to Highway 518
  4. Take Highway 518 East toward Kearney
  5. Turn left on Lakeview Road
  6. Continue 2 km to MysRetreat entrance

Alternative: You can also take Highway 17 East to Highway 11 South for a more scenic forest route.

Nearby Attractions

Discover more of Ontario's natural beauty and local attractions

Algonquin Provincial Park

Algonquin Provincial Park

Distance: 15 minutes drive

Ontario's premier wilderness park featuring extensive canoe routes, wildlife viewing, hiking trails, and visitor education programs.

Don't miss: Wildlife viewing, Visitor Centre, Logging Museum

Arrowhead Provincial Park

Distance: 30 minutes drive

Famous for its frozen marsh boardwalk in winter and beautiful hiking trails year-round. Popular for photography and nature walks.

Best for: Winter activities, boardwalk walks, photography

Arrowhead Provincial Park
Huntsville Downtown

Huntsville

Distance: 45 minutes drive

Charming Muskoka town with shopping, restaurants, art galleries, and cultural attractions. Gateway to the famous Muskoka region.

Highlights: Downtown shops, restaurants, Group of Seven outdoor gallery

Killbear Provincial Park

Distance: 1 hour drive

Stunning Georgian Bay park known for its massive granite rock formations, clear waters, and excellent swimming beaches.

Famous for: Rock formations, swimming, camping, scenic drives

Killbear Provincial Park
Dorset Scenic Lookout Tower

Dorset Scenic Lookout

Distance: 25 minutes drive

Climb the lookout tower for spectacular 360-degree views of Muskoka's lake country. Especially stunning during fall foliage season.

Best time: Fall colors (late September), summer evenings

Muskoka Wildlife Centre

Distance: 40 minutes drive

Educational wildlife rehabilitation centre where you can learn about local wildlife and conservation efforts.

Great for: Families, education, wildlife photography
